3.4 The exponential family

p(y∣ϕ)=h(y)ef(ϕ)⊤T(y)−A(f(ϕ))p(\bold{y}|\phi)=h(\bold{y})e^{f(\phi)^\top\mathcal{T}(\bold{y})-A(f(\phi))}p(y∣ϕ)=h(y)ef(ϕ)⊤T(y)−A(f(ϕ))﻿

  • With
    • T(y)\mathcal{T}(\bold{y})T(y)﻿ the sufficient statistics
    • f(ϕ)=μf(\phi)=\muf(ϕ)=μ﻿ the canonical parameters
    • A(f(ϕ))A(f(\phi)) A(f(ϕ))﻿ is the log partition function
  • canonical form: f(ϕ)=ϕf(\phi)=\phif(ϕ)=ϕ﻿
  • natural exponential family (NEF): T(y)=y\bold{\mathcal{T}(y)=y}T(y)=y﻿

This becomes:

p(y∣ϕ)=h(y)eϕ⊤y−A(ϕ)p(\bold{y}|\phi)=h(\bold{y})e^{\phi^\top \bold{y}-A(\phi)}p(y∣ϕ)=h(y)eϕ⊤y−A(ϕ)﻿

The first and second cumulants of a distribution are E[Y]\mathbb{E}[Y]E[Y]﻿ and V[Y]\mathbb{V}[Y]V[Y]﻿

This first and second moments are E[Y]\mathbb{E}[Y]E[Y]﻿ and E[Y2]\mathbb{E}[Y^2]E[Y2]﻿

Important property of the exponential family: derivatives of the log partition function yields the cumulants of the sufficient statistics

∇A(ϕ)=E[T(y)]\nabla A(\phi)=\mathbb{E}[\mathcal{T(\bold{y})}]∇A(ϕ)=E[T(y)]﻿

∇2A(ϕ)=Cov[T(y)]\nabla^2A(\phi)=Cov[\mathcal{T(\bold{y})}]∇2A(ϕ)=Cov[T(y)]﻿

Since the Hessian is pos-def and the log likelihood has the form ϕ⊤T(y)−A(ϕ)\phi^\top \mathcal{T}(\bold{y})-A(\phi)ϕ⊤T(y)−A(ϕ)﻿, it is concave and hence the MLE has a unique global max.
