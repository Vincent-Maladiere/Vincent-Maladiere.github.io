7.5 Singular Value Decomposition (SVD)

SVD generalizes EVD to rectangular matrices. Even for square matrices, an EVD does not always exist, whereas an SVD always exists.

7.5.1 Basics

Any real m×nm\times nm×n matrix can be decomposed as:

A=USV⊤=σ1[∣u1∣][—v1⊤—]+⋯+σr[∣ur∣][—vr⊤—]A=USV^\top=\sigma_1 \begin{bmatrix}| \\u_1 \\ |\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}— v_1^\top—\end{bmatrix}+\dots+\sigma_r \begin{bmatrix}| \\u_r \\ |\end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix}— v_r^\top—\end{bmatrix}A=USV⊤=σ1​​∣u1​∣​​[—v1⊤​—​]+⋯+σr​​∣ur​∣​​[—vr⊤​—​]

with UUUand VVV orthogonal matrices (left and right singular vectors):

  • UU⊤=ImUU^\top=I_mUU⊤=Im​
  • VV⊤=InVV^\top=I_nVV⊤=In​
  • SSS is a m×nm\times nm×n matrix, containing the rank r=min⁡(m,n)r=\min(m,n)r=min(m,n) singular values σi≥0\sigma_i\geq0σi​≥0.

The cost of computing the SVD is O(min⁡(mn2,m2n))O(\min(mn^2,m^2n))O(min(mn2,m2n))

7.5.2 Connection between EVD and SVD

If AAA is real, symmetric, and positive definite, then the singular values are equal to the eigenvalues:

A=USV⊤=USU⊤A=USV^\top=USU^\topA=USV⊤=USU⊤

More generally, we have:

7.5.3 Pseudo inverse

The Moore-Penrose pseudo-inverse of AAA is denoted A†A^\daggerA† and has the following properties:

AA†A=AA†AA†=A†(AA†)⊤=AA†(A†A)⊤=A†A\begin{align} AA^\dagger A&= A \\ A^\dagger A A^\dagger&=A^\dagger \\ (A A^\dagger)^\top&=A A^\dagger \\ (A^\dagger A)^\top &=A^\dagger A \end{align}AA†AA†AA†(AA†)⊤(A†A)⊤​=A=A†=AA†=A†A​​

If AAA is square and non-singular, then A†=A−1A^\dagger=A^{-1}A†=A−1

If m>nm>nm>n and all columns of AAA are linearly independent (i.e. AAA is full-rank):

A†=(A⊤A)−1A⊤A^\dagger=(A^\top A)^{-1}A^\topA†=(A⊤A)−1A⊤

In this case, A†A^\daggerA† is the left inverse of AAA (but not its right inverse):

A†A=IA^\dagger A=IA†A=I

We can also compute the pseudo-inverse using the SVD:

A†=Vdiag([1/σ1,…,1/σr,0,…,0])U⊤A^\dagger=V \mathrm{diag}(\begin{bmatrix}1/\sigma_1,\dots,1/\sigma_r,0,\dots,0\end{bmatrix} )U^\topA†=Vdiag([1/σ1​,…,1/σr​,0,…,0​])U⊤

When n<mn<mn<m, the right inverse of AAA is:

A†=A⊤(AA⊤)−1A^\dagger=A^\top(AA^\top)^{-1}A†=A⊤(AA⊤)−1

and we have:

AA†=IAA^\dagger=IAA†=I
