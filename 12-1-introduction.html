12.1 Introduction

We previously discussed:
the logistic regression model p(y∣x,w)=Ber(σ(x⊤w))p(y|\bold{x},\bold{w})=\mathrm{Ber}(\sigma(\bold{x}^\top \bold{w}))p(y∣x,w)=Ber(σ(x⊤w))﻿​
the linear regression model p(y∣x,w)=N(y∣x⊤w,σ2)p(y|\bold{x},\bold{w})=\mathcal{N}(y|\bold{x}^\top \bold{w},\sigma^2)p(y∣x,w)=N(y∣x⊤w,σ2)﻿.
For both of the models, the mean of the output E[y∣x,w]\mathbb{E}[y|\bold{x},\bold{w}]E[y∣x,w]﻿ is a linear function of the input x\bold{x}x﻿.
Both models belong to the broader family of generalized linear models (GLM).
A GLM is a conditional version of an exponential family distribution, in which the natural parameters are a linear function of the input:

where:
ηn≜w⊤xn\eta_n\triangleq \bold{w}^\top \bold{x}_nηn​≜w⊤xn​﻿ is the (input dependent) natural parameter
A(ηn)A(\eta_n)A(ηn​)﻿ is the log normalizer
y=T(y)y=\mathcal{T}(y)y=T(y)﻿ is the sufficient statistic
σ2\sigma^2σ2﻿ is the dispersion term
