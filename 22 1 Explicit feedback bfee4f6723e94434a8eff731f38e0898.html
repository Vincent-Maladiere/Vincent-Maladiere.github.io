22.1.1 Datasets

In 2006, Netflix released a large dataset of 100m movie ratings (from a scale of 1 to 5) from 480k users on 18k movies. Despite its large size, the matrix is 99% sparse (unknown).

The winning team used a combination of techniques, see the Bellkor solution to the Netflix prize and Lessons from the Netflix Challenge.

The Netflix dataset is not available anymore, but the MovieLens group have released an anonymized public dataset of movie ratings, on a scale of 1-5, that can be used for research. There are also the Jester jokes and the BookCrossing datasets.
