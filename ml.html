Non Linear SVM
The training data can be rarely separated using an hyperplane
Chance is more for a non-linear separable data in lower-dimensional space to become linear separable in higher-dimensional space.
In general, it is possible to map points in a d-dimensional space to some D-dimensional spaceÂ to check the possibility of linear separability
The new model has to include both the old requirement of finding the hyperplane that gives the biggest margin and the new one of generalizing the training data correctly by not allowing too many classification errors.
For example, one could think of minimizing the same quantity plus a constant times the number of misclassification errors in the training data
minâˆ£âˆ£Î²âˆ£âˆ£2+C(misclassificationâ€…â€Šerror)min||\beta||^2+C(misclassification\;error)minâˆ£âˆ£Î²âˆ£âˆ£2+C(misclassificationerror)ï»¿
A better solution will take into account theÂ distance of the misclassified samples to their correct decision regions
minâˆ£âˆ£Î²âˆ£âˆ£2+C(distanceâ€…â€Šofâ€…â€Šmisclassifiedâ€…â€Šsamplesâ€…â€Štoâ€…â€Štheirâ€…â€Šcorrectâ€…â€Šregions)min||\beta||^2+C(distance\; of\; misclassified\; samples\; to\; their\; correct\; regions)minâˆ£âˆ£Î²âˆ£âˆ£2+C(distanceofmisclassifiedsamplestotheircorrectregions)ï»¿
minÎ²,Î²0L(Î²)=âˆ£âˆ£Î²âˆ£âˆ£2+Câˆ‘iÎ¾imin_{\beta, \beta_0}L(\beta)=||\beta||^2+C\sum_i \xi_iminÎ²,Î²0â€‹â€‹L(Î²)=âˆ£âˆ£Î²âˆ£âˆ£2+Câˆ‘iâ€‹Î¾iâ€‹ï»¿
subject to
yi(Î²Txi+Î²0)>1âˆ’Î¾iy_i(\beta^Tx_i+\beta_0)>1-\xi_iyiâ€‹(Î²Txiâ€‹+Î²0â€‹)>1âˆ’Î¾iâ€‹ï»¿ and Î¾i>0\xi_i>0Î¾iâ€‹>0ï»¿
Choose C
Large values give solutions with less classification errors but smaller margin
Small values of C give solutions focusing more on the hyperplane, without much importance for classification errors, so wider margin

Kmeans
Basic intuition
Randomly choose 2 centroids, C1C_1C1â€‹ï»¿ and C2C_2C2â€‹ï»¿
Calculate distance for each from both centroid. Closer to C1C_1C1â€‹ï»¿ are labeled 000ï»¿, o.w labeled 111ï»¿
Next, compute the average for blue and red points and update the centroids
Iterate over 2 and 3 until convergence (or stopping criteria on number of iteration, or precision) is reach
The distances between test data and their centroids are minimum
minâ€…â€Š[J=âˆ‘redsd(C1,red)+âˆ‘bluesd(C2,blue)]min \;[J=\sum_{reds}d(C_1, red)+\sum_{blues}d(C_2, blue)]min[J=âˆ‘redsâ€‹d(C1â€‹,red)+âˆ‘bluesâ€‹d(C2â€‹,blue)]ï»¿
